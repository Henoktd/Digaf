Section 1

Backup and Disaster Recovery

What is backed up, and how often

ItemDetail
FrequencyEvery night at 02:00 (Africa/Addis_Ababa time), automatically, with no manual step required
ScopeA complete export of the registry database — every shareholder, shareholding, transfer, certificate, KYC record, lien, legal hold, approval, and audit log entry
Local retentionKept on the hosting server for 14 days on a rolling basis; older local copies are automatically removed
Offsite copyEach nightly backup is additionally sent as an email attachment to a dedicated mailbox independent of the hosting server, so a copy survives even total loss of the server itself
Integrity checkEvery backup is verified to be a non-trivial size immediately after creation; a suspiciously small or failed backup is flagged rather than silently kept

Recovery procedure

In the event the platform or its database is lost, corrupted, or otherwise needs to be restored:

1
Identify the most recent good backupLocal backups are on the server at /home/digaf/backups; the offsite copy is in the designated mailbox, one email per night, most recent at the top.
2
Provision a databaseA fresh PostgreSQL instance is started (on the existing server if the database alone was affected, or on a new server if the whole VM was lost).
3
Restore the backupThe chosen backup file is loaded into the new database using the standard PostgreSQL restore command, recreating every table exactly as it was at backup time.
4
Point the application at the restored databaseThe platform's configuration is updated to connect to the restored database, and the application is restarted.
5
VerifyAn administrator logs in, confirms the shareholder count and a sample of recent transactions match expectations, and checks the audit log for the last recorded actions before the incident.
Recovery Point Objective (RPO): up to 24 hours of data (the time since the last nightly backup) in the worst case. Recovery Time Objective (RTO): the restore procedure above is designed to be completed within a few hours by someone with server access and basic PostgreSQL familiarity.

Recommended: periodic restore test

A backup that has never been restored is unverified. It is recommended that the restore procedure above be performed at least once, against a throwaway test database, to confirm it works end to end — not merely that backup files exist.

Section 2

Data Retention Policy

The shareholder register is a statutory corporate record. This section states how long the platform retains it and related governance data.

Record typeRetentionBasis
Shareholder register (shareholders, shareholdings, transfers, certificates) Retained permanently — no automatic deletion. Records remain even after a shareholder disposes of all shares, preserving historical ownership Statutory register requirement
KYC records and identity documents Retained permanently alongside the associated shareholder record AML/KYC recordkeeping practice
Liens, pledges, and legal holds (including released/lifted ones) Retained permanently — a released lien or lifted hold remains visible in its historical state, never deleted Legal traceability
Audit log Retained permanently; the log is append-only and cannot be edited or deleted through the application Audit trail integrity
Database backups 14 days on the hosting server (rolling); the offsite emailed copy persists in the recipient mailbox subject to that mailbox's own retention settings Operational recovery window
User account access logs (sign-ins) Retained by the authentication provider for the life of the account Security monitoring
Principle: nothing in the shareholder register or audit trail is ever hard-deleted by the platform. Corrections are made by recording a new, approved change — never by editing or removing history. This is what allows the audit log to remain a complete and trustworthy record.

Review

This policy should be reviewed by Legal & Compliance annually, or whenever NBE retention requirements for shareholder registers or AML records are updated.
