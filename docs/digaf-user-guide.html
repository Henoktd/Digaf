Introduction

What is the Digaf Platform?

The Digaf Shareholder Registry Platform is a secure, web-based system for managing Digaf's complete shareholder records. It covers everything from the initial registration of shareholders to KYC compliance checks, share certificate generation, dividend tracking, and audit logging.

All records are stored in a central database accessible only to authorised users. Every action taken in the system is logged for compliance and audit purposes.

What can the platform do?

👥
Shareholder Registry Maintain complete records of all shareholders including personal details, shares held, and transfer history.
📋
KYC Compliance Track identity verification status for each shareholder and manage compliance documents.
📜
Share Certificates Generate and print official share certificates for shareholders with one click.
📊
Cap Table View a real-time breakdown of share ownership, classes, and capital structure.
💰
Dividends Record and track dividend distributions to shareholders.
📁
Audit Log Every action in the system is logged with the user, date, and details for full traceability.
🔄
Share Transfers Record the transfer of shares between shareholders with full history.
👤
User Management Create user accounts, assign roles, and control who can access what in the system.

Getting Started

First Login & Setting Your Password

When your account is created by the administrator, you will receive a welcome email containing your temporary password. Follow the steps below to log in and set your own password.

1
Open your welcome email You will receive an email from digaf-noreply@sol-ventures.com with your login credentials: the platform URL, your email address, and a temporary password.
2
Go to the platform URL Open a browser and navigate to http://196.190.220.15
3
Sign in with your temporary password Enter your email address and the temporary password from the welcome email, then click Sign In.
4
Set your own password The platform will immediately ask you to set a new password. Choose something secure (at least 8 characters). You will not be able to use the platform until this step is complete.
5
You're in After setting your password you will be taken to the main dashboard. Your temporary password is now deactivated.
Forgot your password? Contact your system administrator to reset it. They can set a new password for your account from the User Management section.

Changing your password later

To change your password at any time after your first login, go to:
http://196.190.220.15/auth/update-password

Digaf User Guide  ·  User Roles
Access Control

User Roles & Permissions

Every user in the platform is assigned a role that controls what they can see and do. Roles are assigned by the Governance Admin when your account is created.

Role What they can do
Governance Admin Full access to all features. Can create and manage users, change settings, and perform all operations.
Maker Can create and submit records (shareholders, transfers, certificates) for review and approval.
Checker 1 Reviews and approves records submitted by the Maker. First level of approval.
Checker 2 Second level of approval for records already approved by Checker 1.
Compliance Officer Manages KYC verification and compliance-related actions. Can view all records.
Viewer Read-only access. Can view records and reports but cannot make any changes.
Your role is shown in the sidebar when you are logged in. If you need a different level of access, contact your Governance Admin.
Digaf User Guide  ·  Key Features
Core Features

Shareholders, Certificates & KYC

Viewing Shareholders

Go to Shareholders in the left sidebar to see the full list of registered shareholders. You can search by name or ID number. Click on any shareholder's name to open their full profile, which shows their personal details, shares held, transfer history, KYC status, and certificates.

Adding a New Shareholder

1
Click "+ Add Shareholder" This button is at the top right of the Shareholders page.
2
Fill in the shareholder's details Enter their full name (English and Amharic), ID number, address, and share details.
3
Submit for approval The record goes through the Maker → Checker 1 → Checker 2 approval workflow before it is finalised.

Generating a Share Certificate

Open a shareholder's profile and click "Generate Certificate". The certificate is produced instantly in PDF format and can be printed directly from the browser. The certificate includes the shareholder's name, share details, and the company's authorised/subscribed/paid-up capital.

KYC Compliance

The KYC section shows the verification status of each shareholder. A Compliance Officer can mark shareholders as verified, pending, or requiring additional documents. The status is visible on the shareholder's profile and on the KYC dashboard.

Bulk Import: If you have a large number of shareholders to add, they can be imported all at once from an Excel file using the Imports section. Contact the administrator for the correct file format.

Administration

Settings & Support

Company Settings

The Settings page (accessible to Governance Admin only) is where the company's capital information is configured. This information appears on share certificates and reports.

  • Authorized Capital — the maximum capital the company is authorised to issue
  • Subscribed Capital — the amount shareholders have agreed to pay
  • Paid-up Capital — the amount actually paid by shareholders
  • Par Value — the face value of each individual share
  • Company Address — City, Sub-City, Woreda, House No., P.O. Box

User Management

Governance Admins can go to Users in the sidebar to:

  • Create new user accounts (a welcome email with a temporary password is sent automatically)
  • Assign or change a user's role
  • Reset a user's password directly
  • Remove a user from the platform

Audit Log

Every action performed in the system — creating a record, approving a transfer, generating a certificate — is recorded in the Audit Log with the user's name, the date and time, and what was changed. This log cannot be edited or deleted.
