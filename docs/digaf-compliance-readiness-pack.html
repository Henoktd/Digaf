What this document is

This pack supports the review requested before the Digaf Shareholder Registry Platform is adopted as the company's official shareholder register. It maps each area of regulatory and governance obligation to the specific control or feature in the platform that addresses it, states plainly what is already operational, and is explicit about anything still in progress.

This document does not substitute for Legal & Compliance's own assessment against the current text of NBE directives and the Commercial Code. It is a working aid to make that review faster — every claim here should be independently verified against the applicable regulation by the reviewing team.

Important: this document describes the platform's technical and procedural capabilities. It is not a legal opinion. Specific NBE directive numbers and thresholds (ownership concentration limits, fit-and-proper criteria, reporting formats, etc.) should be confirmed by Legal & Compliance against the current regulatory text, as these are updated periodically by the National Bank of Ethiopia.

Section 2

Governance Controls — Segregation of Duties

Sound governance practice, and NBE expectations for regulated financial institutions generally, call for segregation of duties on any action that changes ownership or compliance records — no single person should be able to both create and approve a change unchecked.

The maker-checker control

The platform implements a maker-checker control on share transfers and other governance actions: a Maker submits a change, which requires approval by a Checker (or, for transfers, sequential approval by Checker 1 then Checker 2) before it takes effect. No individual can approve their own submission — this is enforced at the application layer, not just by convention.

RoleFunctionStatus
Governance AdminFull administrative access; manages users, roles, and company settings; acts as approver for liens and legal holdsActive
MakerCreates and submits shareholder, transfer, certificate, lien, and legal hold recordsActive
Checker 1First-level review and approval of submitted transfer recordsActive
Checker 2Final approval before a transfer record takes effectActive
Compliance OfficerManages KYC verification and compliance status independently of transaction processingActive
ViewerRead-only access for board members and auditorsActive

SLA and escalation controls

Each approval stage carries a configurable target turnaround time (default 5 business days for transfers). Items exceeding this target are flagged as overdue on the dashboard and escalate to the assigned recipient role — preventing pending approvals from stalling unnoticed.

Section 7

Operating Procedures Recommended for Formal Adoption

A compliant registry is a combination of software controls and documented business process. The following short procedures are recommended for Digaf to formally approve, even briefly, so the platform's technical controls are backed by an on-paper policy:

  1. User access procedure — who may request a new platform account, who approves the request (Governance Admin), and the process for revoking access when someone leaves their role.
  2. Shareholder registration procedure — required documents before a new shareholder is registered, and who verifies them (Compliance Officer) before KYC is marked verified.
  3. Share transfer approval procedure — formalizes the existing Maker → Checker 1 → Checker 2 flow as an approved business process, naming who may hold each role.
  4. Lien/pledge and legal hold procedure — names who may act as Maker and who as the approving Governance Admin for registering or releasing a lien, and for imposing or lifting a legal hold.
  5. Record correction procedure — how an error in the registry is corrected once approved, ensuring corrections are also logged and approved rather than edited silently.
  6. Data retention and backup policy — states the retention period for registry records and confirms the nightly backup and (once complete) offsite copy procedure.
  7. Incident response procedure — who is notified and what steps are taken if unauthorized access or a data issue is suspected.
Each of these can be a single page. Digaf's Legal & Compliance team may already have templates for several of these from other systems — the goal is consistency with the platform's actual technical behaviour, not new invention.
